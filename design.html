~ About

Map-first by design: the app opens on the map so context is instant - where you are, what’s around, and how soon it starts. Filters for date, distance and type are one tap away, recommendations adapt to your interests, and a short onboarding gathers just the essentials to personalize without friction.

  • Role: UX/UI, prototyping, visual identity
  • Artifacts: low-fi wireframes, user flows, use/edge cases

~ UX / UI

Mapped key journeys and edge cases (venue closed, tickets sold out, location denied) to keep states predictable and resilient.

Usability testing

I ran quick sessions on the core flows and collected feedback via Google Forms. The findings refined copy, filter clarity, and the map↔list handoff.

Onboarding flow (survey results)

What we changed after testing

From 9 quick responses we highlighted few problematic points: progress clarity, the interests list felt heavy, privacy sharing hesitations, a couple of bugs, and a wish for smoother motion.

    Clear steps: Added simple 3-step indicator.
    Lighter interests: Grouped options, added a “all” interests to choose option to avoid overwhelment.
    Smoother feel: Small transitions for success/error and screen changes.
    Bug fix: Fixed the glitches found in testing.

Result It’s clearer where you are, what’s needed, and why we ask for info. Errors are earlier and clearer; the flow feels faster and calmer.

About

I created the visual identity and UX/UI for an internal learning platform that helps new joiners - and people moving between teams - get up to speed quickly. Colleagues can contribute resources and create short courses; the community can review, comment, and flag outdated items to keep quality high.

Before design, I interviewed people across departments to understand needs and expectations. Those insights shaped navigation, contribution flows, and moderation so the platform stays useful and trustworthy.

  • Role: Identity, UX/UI, prototyping
  • Artifacts: interviews, wireframes, flows, design system
